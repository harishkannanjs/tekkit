Everything You Need To Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Which course should I take?

If youre completely new to building websites or AI-assisted development, start with our free intro resources to understand the basics of vibe coding. If you already have some experience with HTML, CSS, or simple frontend builds and want to accelerate your skills using structured workflow strategies, prompts, and real-world projects, apply to our Vibe Coding Mastery. If youre already comfortable with core frontend concepts and want to push your creative and technical limits, youll benefit most from our advanced vibe-coding pathways as they release. Each one focuses on deepening your understanding of design systems, project flow, and AI-powered build optimization not just tools.

Who will teach the course?

All Tekkit bootcamps are taught by Harish Kannan J S and expert mentors who specialize in turning beginners into confident builders. Our instructors focus on clarity, hands-on practice, and real project refinement rather than overwhelming you with unnecessary theory.

Do I have to apply right before my intended start date?

Not at all. Many students apply weeks in advance to secure a seat in the upcoming cohort. If you apply too close to the start date, the batch might already be filled, and you may have to join the following one. Tekkit cohorts run at regular intervals, and early applicants get priority access, plus pre-course materials to warm up before classes begin.

Where can I find the curriculum?

Our core curriculum outline is publicly available on the Tekkit website. We keep the deeper modules, workflows, and guided exercises exclusive to enrolled students so you get a structured, high-quality learning experience. The bootcamp isnt just about reading content its about applying it. Instructors challenge your understanding, refine your approach, and ensure you develop real-world ability through hands-on builds and guided feedback.

Is it online or in person?

All Tekkit bootcamps are fully online. You can learn from anywhere, join live sessions, and participate in community discussions without geographic limitations.

How much time per week?

Most students complete the weekly workload in 5 to 7 hours, including live classes. The pace is designed to fit a students or working professionals schedule while still offering consistent progress through structured assignments and project sessions
